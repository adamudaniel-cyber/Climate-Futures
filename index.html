Climate Futures: Monte Carlo and Regime-Switching Framework for Smarter Climate Scenario Analysis

Developed from the postgraduate research:
"Monte Carlo and Regime-Switching Models for Global Climate Change Scenario Analysis and Sustainable Decision-Making"

Introduction

Climate Futures is an open research initiative that develops advanced statistical models capable of generating thousands of realistic climate scenarios.

By combining historical climate observations with Monte Carlo simulation and Regime-Switching models, the project helps transform climate uncertainty into practical knowledge that supports evidence-based planning and sustainable decision-making.

Rather than predicting one future, Climate Futures explores many possible futures, helping decision-makers better understand climate risks, uncertainty, and resilience.

About the Project

Understanding Climate Uncertainty Through Advanced Statistical Modeling
Climate Futures is an innovative research initiative designed to improve the way climate uncertainty is analyzed and communicated.

Traditional climate forecasts often provide a single projected outcome. However, the climate system is complex and influenced by many interacting factors that can change unexpectedly.

Climate Futures addresses this challenge by combining advanced stochastic modeling with Monte Carlo simulation to generate thousands of plausible climate scenarios. This probabilistic approach provides a more comprehensive understanding of future climate conditions and supports informed planning under uncertainty.

The project was developed from postgraduate research in Statistics and demonstrates how modern statistical methods can contribute to climate resilience, sustainability, and evidence-based environmental decision-making.

Why Climate Futures?

Climate uncertainty affects everyone.

Whether planning cities, protecting agriculture, managing water resources, or preparing for disasters, decisions made today depend on understanding tomorrow's climate.

Climate Futures helps answer questions such as:

  • How uncertain is the future climate?
  • What extreme climate conditions are possible?
  • How can uncertainty be measured?
  • How can statistical models improve climate planning?
  • How can simulation support sustainable development?

Instead of relying on one forecast, Climate Futures explores thousands of possible futures, allowing better preparation for uncertainty.

How It Works

Climate Futures combines historical climate observations with modern statistical techniques through the following steps:

  1. Collection of global climate data.
  2. Data cleaning and preprocessing.
  3. Detection of hidden climate regimes using Hidden Markov Models.
  4. Climate dynamics modeled using Regime-Switching Geometric Brownian Motion.
  5. Generation of thousands of future climate scenarios using Monte Carlo simulation.
  6. Analysis of uncertainty, risk, and climate variability.
  7. Production of decision-support information for sustainable planning.

Research Objectives

The research has successfully achieved the following key milestones:

  • Developed an innovative probabilistic framework for climate scenario analysis.
  • Improved the mathematical representation of climate uncertainty.
  • Simulated thousands of consistent future climate pathways.
  • Quantified climate risks and volatility shifts using advanced statistical methods.
  • Supported evidence-based climate adaptation and resilience planning.
  • Promoted sustainable decision-making through open scientific research.

Methodology

Climate Futures integrates several advanced statistical methods:

  • Historical Climate Data
  • Hidden Markov Models (HMM)
  • Regime-Switching Geometric Brownian Motion (RS-GBM)
  • Monte Carlo Simulation
  • Euler-Maruyama Numerical Approximation
  • Maximum Likelihood Estimation
  • Risk Metrics (Value-at-Risk and Expected Shortfall)

These methods work together to generate thousands of statistically consistent climate scenarios.

Results

The framework demonstrates how probabilistic climate modeling provides richer insights than traditional deterministic forecasts.

Key outputs include:

  • Thousands of simulated climate futures.
  • Climate uncertainty estimates.
  • Extreme-event probabilities.
  • Risk metrics.
  • Scenario distributions.
  • Climate regime probabilities.

These results help users better understand both expected climate trends and potential extreme outcomes.

The Researcher

Daniel Adamu

Daniel Adamu is a researcher, lecturer, and actuarial specialist with multidisciplinary expertise in statistics, stochastic modeling, risk analysis, and sustainable development. His academic and professional qualifications include a BSc in Mathematics, an MSc in Actuarial Science, an MSc in Leadership Studies, an MSc in International Relations and Diplomatic Studies (in view), a PGD in Education, a Diploma in French Language, and a PhD in Statistics (in view).

Daniel lectures in Actuarial Science at the University of Jos, Nigeria, and previously taught at Kampala International University. Before academia, he held technical and risk roles in insurance. A Fellow of several institutes, he presents research across Africa and Asia.

His research spans stochastic modeling, Monte Carlo simulation, climate scenario analysis, and pension systems. Through Climate Futures, he develops open tools making advanced climate modeling accessible, helping build resilience to future climate risks. He is also an author and songwriter.
